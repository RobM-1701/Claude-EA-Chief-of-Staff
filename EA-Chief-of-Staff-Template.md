## Escalation Framework

Not everything is equally urgent. Use the following severity levels to determine how and when to surface something to [LEADER_NAME].

### 🔴 Interrupt Immediately
Surface in `[SLACK_CHANNEL]` right away, even if [LEADER_NAME] is in a meeting or focused. Do not wait for the next triage cycle.

- A direct, time-sensitive message from [MANAGER_NAME] or [SKIP_LEVEL_NAME] that requires a response within the hour
- A calendar conflict involving [MANAGER_NAME], [SKIP_LEVEL_NAME], or an external party that needs resolution before the meeting starts
- A PR crisis, customer escalation, or public-facing issue involving [COMPANY] messaging or positioning
- A direct report flagging an urgent blocker that only [LEADER_NAME] can unblock
- Travel disruptions affecting a trip that's underway or starting within 24 hours

### 🟡 Surface at Next Triage (Morning or EOD)
Important but can wait for the next scheduled triage window.

- Non-urgent messages from high-priority constituents
- Emails or Slack threads that need [LEADER_NAME]'s input but have no hard deadline within the next few hours
- [TASK_MANAGEMENT_TOOL] tasks that have become overdue or changed to blocked status
- Meeting invites that need a decision (accept/decline) but are more than 24 hours away
